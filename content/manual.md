# Manual — Skills, Combat, and the World

Lost City Singleplayer Progressive runs the authentic 2004 RuneScape engine. Everything documented here applies equally to your character and to the AI bots — they follow the same game mechanics you do.

## The Skill System

All 19 skills from the 2004 era are active and fully progressive.

**Combat Skills (7):** Attack, Strength, Defence, Magic, Range, Prayer, Hitpoints. Bots train these through real combat — slayer-style tasks in the Wilderness, ranging from Chaos Druids at level 1 through to player-killing at higher levels.

**Gathering Skills (4):** Fishing, Woodcutting, Mining, Runecrafting. Bots gather at the optimal locations for their level — willows at Draynor, coal at the Mining Guild, essence at the Zamorak wizard teleport locations.

**Artisan Skills (6):** Firemaking, Fletching, Smithing, Cooking, Crafting, Herblore. Processed from gathered resources. Bots bank at Varrock West, Falador East, and Draynor depending on what they are processing.

**Miscellaneous Skills (2):** Agility and Thieving. Bots train Agility on the Gnome Stronghold course and advance to Barbarian Outpost and Wilderness courses at appropriate levels. Thieving progresses from men and women through to paladins and knights.

## Combat and PvP

The Wilderness is active. Bots enter the Wilderness for Runecrafting at the Abyss, for Agility training, and for deliberate PvP hunting. Combat level brackets are respected — bots will not attack players outside their combat range unless provoked.

Prayer is used situationally. Protection prayers are activated in dangerous situations. Bots bring food appropriate to their Hitpoints level.

## Navigation and Pathfinding

Bots use optimal pathfinding with full gate, door, and obstacle awareness. Al Kharid toll is paid automatically. Karamja boat trips are taken correctly. Bots will not get stuck on barriers that would confuse simpler pathing systems.

## Economy and Trading

A dynamic economy operates between bots. Items are bought and sold at approximate street prices from the 2004 era. Bots will offer trades to players standing near banks and markets. Be warned — some bots are scripted to attempt scams, staying true to the social ecology of the original game.
