# Wilderness PvP

The Wilderness is fully active in 2004SP Progressive. Bots enter, fight, and die — just as players do.

## How Wilderness PvP Works

Bots respect the combat level bracket system. A bot will not attack a player or another bot outside their reachable combat range. Bots that enter the Wilderness for skilling purposes (Runecrafting via the Abyss, Agility training) assess risk before entering and bring food appropriate to their Hitpoints level.

Combat-focus bots actively hunt targets in the Wilderness, prioritising players and bots within their bracket, using protection prayers situationally, and executing weapon specials at the correct HP thresholds.

## Protection Prayers

Bots activate protection prayers when appropriate:
- **Protect from Melee** when attacked by a melee-focused opponent
- **Protect from Magic** against mages using high-level spells
- **Protect from Missiles** against rangers

Prayer points are managed — bots will retreat to recharge rather than fight on empty prayer.

## Special Attacks

Bots carry spec weapons appropriate to their combat level. Dragon dagger (p++) specs are executed at correct HP thresholds. Bots switch to spec weapons mid-fight without hesitation.

## Pking Culture

Some bots are specifically scripted as player-killers. They patrol Edgeville, the 13-20 wild zone near the Chaos Altar, and high-level Wilderness near the King Black Dragon lair. They will attack on sight, attempt to kill, and loot the grave.

This creates a realistic risk environment — the Wilderness feels dangerous the same way it did in 2004 because the threat is genuine, not cosmetic.

## Clan Wars and Multi-zone

Multi-combat zones are respected. Bots will not lone-wolf in multi-zones without allies. Coordinated bot groups can be configured to clan-war against each other in the multi-combat areas of the Wilderness.
